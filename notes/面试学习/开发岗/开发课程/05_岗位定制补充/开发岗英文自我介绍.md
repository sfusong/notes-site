# Development Track Self-Introduction in English

This note is meant for direct speaking in interviews.  
The goal is not to sound overly formal. The goal is to sound clear, confident and believable.

Try to keep the structure stable:

1. who you are  
2. what you do now  
3. what your strongest experience is  
4. why you want this role

---

## 1. 30-second version

Hello, my name is Shifusong. I am currently working in fintech at a wealth management company, mainly on internal financial systems related to asset management and TA workflows. My experience includes product lifecycle systems, TA automation testing, pre-batch warning and audit checks, interface integration and production rollout support. I am interested in developer roles because I want to keep growing on the engineering side while staying close to real financial business systems.

---

## 2. Standard 1-minute version

Hello, my name is Shifusong. I am currently working in fintech at a wealth management company. My work is mainly related to internal business systems, especially asset-management product systems, TA-related workflows, interface integration, UAT support, rollout coordination and some automation-related projects.

From a project perspective, one of my representative projects is a product lifecycle management system, which is more about turning business rules into backend system logic. I have also been involved in TA automation testing and a pre-batch warning and audit platform, which are more related to operational control, regression validation and delivery support. On the technical side, I have mainly worked with Java, Spring Boot, Python, React, SQL and Linux-based environments.

I am interested in developer roles because I believe my background fits positions that require both engineering delivery and understanding of real financial systems. I would like to continue growing in system building and software development within financial institutions.

Hello, my name is Shifusong. I am currently working in a fintech team at a wealth management company. ## 4. Architecture-minded interviewer version

Hello, my name is Shifusong. I am currently working in fintech at a wealth management company, mainly on internal business systems that support asset-management products and TA-related workflows. My work is not only about implementing isolated features. It is more about helping move systems from business requirements into production-ready workflows, including system coordination, interface integration, UAT support, rollout activities and post-release issue closing.

From a technical and architectural perspective, one of my representative projects is a product lifecycle management system, where the key challenge was to translate business rules, calendars and event-triggering logic into stable backend system behavior. I have also worked on TA automation testing and a pre-batch warning and audit platform, which are closer to operational control, batch-process reliability and front-loaded issue detection. Technically, my main line is Java and Spring Boot, and I have also worked with Python, React, SQL and Linux-based delivery environments.

I believe this background fits roles that require not only coding, but also understanding of system boundaries, data flow, operational control and engineering delivery in real financial environments.

This version is especially suitable for interviewers who care about architecture, system thinking and delivery maturity.

## 5. Short and safer version for English interviews

Hello, my name is Shifusong. I am currently working on fintech-related projects in a wealth management company. My experience is strongest in internal financial systems, especially product systems, TA workflows, integration tasks and delivery support. Technically, I have mainly worked with Java, Spring Boot, Python, React and SQL. I am interested in this role because it matches both my domain background and my engineering direction.

This version is especially useful if you want to sound steady and avoid overexplaining at the beginning.

## 6. If they ask “What makes your profile different?”

You can say:

What makes my profile different is that my experience is closely tied to real financial business systems rather than only isolated technical modules. I have worked on asset-management and TA-related workflows, and I have also been involved in interface integration, UAT support, rollout coordination and automation-related work. So I believe my strength is that I can explain both the technical side and the delivery context of financial systems.

---

## 7. If they ask “Why do you want a developer role?”

You can say:

I want to continue in developer roles because I find that I am most motivated when I am building real systems to solve business problems. Compared with doing only coordination work, I prefer to stay close to engineering implementation, system delivery and technical problem solving. Financial institutions are especially interesting to me because the systems are closely tied to real operational workflows and risk-sensitive business processes.

---

## 8. If the interviewer clearly cares about systems and architecture

You can add one sentence like this:

“What I am strongest at is not only describing a single feature, but explaining how a system works across business rules, interface interaction, data flow, batch timing and production delivery.”

Or:

“Many of the projects I worked on were valuable not only because of coding itself, but because they made critical business workflows more controlled, traceable and deliverable.”

---

- Let me give a short overview first.
- From my current experience, I would describe it this way.
- My role was mainly on the engineering and delivery side.
- The core value of that project was...
- I would position that project as...

---

## 10. Do not overclaim

Try not to start with lines like:

- I independently built the entire system.
- I led the whole platform from end to end.
- I was the sole core developer for everything.

A more realistic positioning is:

`a fintech developer candidate with real financial-system experience, practical delivery exposure, and a strong interest in growing further on the engineering side.`

---

I am a fintech developer candidate with real asset-management and TA system experience, practical exposure to integration and delivery, and hands-on work with Java, Spring Boot, Python, React and SQL in financial business contexts.
